Defunct Projects

Raw half-formed ideas, and the ones that did NOT work out. No spin.

Killed
DebateDaily Dead
Domain: Web App / Civic Tech Stack: HTML · CSS · JS Window: 2023 – 2024
Daily structured debate prompts — one topic, two sides, timed rounds. Built to keep formal argumentation sharp outside a school context. The friction of finding a partner and a topic was real; the intent was to remove it.
Post-Mortem

The core problem was retention without stakes. Structured debate only works when both parties are invested — solo drill tools don't replicate that. Built the UI, got the prompt engine running, used it about four times personally. Every session felt like shadowboxing in an empty gym. Competing priorities at school finished it off. The idea isn't wrong; the execution context was.

  • Prompt engine and timer worked correctly
  • Solo use case never justified the overhead
  • No traction, no iteration loop, no reason to continue
Appalachian Manufacturing Co. Dead
Domain: Physical Product / Fabrication Stack: Ender 3 · Cameo 2 · Inkscape · Manual Fab Window: 2022 – 2024
Small-batch goods out of the NC High Country — 3D printed parts, vinyl-cut stickers, small custom hardware runs. "Appalachian" was doing real work in the branding: things made here, by hand, in the mountains, not drop-shipped.
Post-Mortem

Didn't survive contact with the unit economics. Small-batch physical goods have real COGs: filament, vinyl stock, time, packaging, shipping. Margins on anything priced to actually sell are thin enough that the throughput required is incompatible with a one-person shop that has three other things going on. The fabrication capability still exists — it feeds directly into Activism Motorsports and personal projects — but as a standalone revenue operation, it didn't work. FishStix Creatives absorbed the vinyl/sticker portion; the rest quietly dissolved.

  • Product quality was fine — economics weren't
  • Throughput requirements incompatible with current capacity
  • Sticker / vinyl work spun off into FishStix Creatives
  • Fabrication capability preserved and redirected, not lost
FishSTIX Creatives Dead
Domain: Design / Print / Vinyl Stack: Cameo 2 · Inkscape · Silhouette Studio Window: 2024 – 2025
Stickers, graphics, and assorted small-batch visual experiments. Spun out of Appalachian Manufacturing Co. to give the vinyl and print work its own identity. The idea was to keep the creative output separate from the fabrication side and build a small catalog of original designs.
Post-Mortem

Inherited the same structural problem as its parent: the economics of small-batch physical goods don't work at low volume. A sticker shop needs either a large catalog with consistent demand or a connected audience to sell into — FishSTIX had neither in sufficient quantity. The design capability lives on inside Activism Motorsports and personal projects. The storefront does not.

  • Design work and toolchain preserved — still in active use elsewhere
  • Storefront economics never closed
  • Absorbed back into the broader fabrication context

DPAC — Public Web Tool Waiting on Research
Domain: Web App / Motorsports Stack: HTML · CSS · JS Window: 2024 – Present
Drivetrain-Aware Performance Analytics Calculator — a normalization tool for autocross and hillclimb that accounts for drivetrain config, tire classification, elevation, and power-to-weight. Not an official classing tool; an honest attempt to make lap time comparisons more meaningful than PAX alone.
Blocker

The calculator exists and works. A polished public-facing interface does not. The PAX critique writeup and the elevation weighting model are both still in progress — the public tool is waiting on the research being stable enough to stand behind. Keeps getting deprioritized behind active competition season work.

  • Core normalization formula: functional
  • FWD/RWD delta coefficients need more event data
  • PAX critique writeup in progress
  • Public release blocked on research stability
